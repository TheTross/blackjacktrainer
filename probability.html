Key Ideas in Plain Language

Probability in blackjack does not predict what will happen in the next hand. It describes what tends to happen over many, many hands when the same decisions are repeated in similar situations.

  • House edge: Over the long run, the rules of blackjack give the casino a small built‚Äëin advantage.
  • Expectation: Even a ‚Äúgood‚Äù decision can lose money in the short term because outcomes are still random.
  • Variance: Short streaks of wins or losses are normal noise, not proof that a pattern has changed.
  • Discipline: The only part you control is how you choose to act and when you choose to stop.

The goal of this module is to make those ideas feel concrete, so the numbers you see at the table are less surprising and less emotional.

How to Read Variance

When you first see simulations, it can be tempting to treat the best‚Äëcase paths as goals. A more disciplined view focuses on the range, not the peak.

  • Expect swings in both directions, even with consistent, reasonable decisions.
  • Measure sessions by whether you respected your limits, not by a single result.
  • Remember that no model changes the underlying house advantage.
