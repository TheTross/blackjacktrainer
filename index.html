Blackjack Basics
Learn the core rules and a simplified strategy, then jump back into the trainer to drill it.[web:58][web:63][web:66]

How the game works

  • Goal: Get closer to 21 than the dealer without going over.
  • Cards: 2–10 = face value, J/Q/K = 10, Aces = 1 or 11.[web:66]
  • You act first. If you bust, you lose immediately, even if the dealer later busts.[web:63]
  • Dealer usually hits until at least 17 (house rules vary slightly).[web:63][web:64]

Your basic options

  • Hit – take another card.
  • Stand – keep your hand and end your turn.[web:60][web:66]
  • (Advanced: double, split, surrender – covered in a future version.)

Simplified starter strategy

  • Always split Aces and 8s.[web:57][web:58]
  • Hit totals of 8 or less every time.[web:57][web:64]
  • Stand on 12–16 when dealer shows 2–6 (weak upcard).[web:57][web:58]
  • Hit until at least 17 when dealer shows 7–Ace (strong upcard).[web:57][web:63]
Table Etiquette
Don’t just play better – look like you know what you’re doing. These are the unwritten rules most beginners never learn.[web:56][web:63]

Before you sit down

  • Buy in when the dealer finishes a round, not mid-hand.
  • Place cash on the felt; don’t hand it directly to the dealer.[web:56]
  • Check the table limits and rules (blackjack payout, number of decks).[web:63]

During the hand

  • Use hand signals (tap for hit, wave for stand) so cameras can see your decisions.[web:56][web:63]
  • Don’t touch your chips once the first card has been dealt.
  • Keep your cards on the table in games where cards are dealt face up.[web:56]

How to be a good player

  • Don’t blame other players for “taking the dealer’s bust card” – variance happens.
  • Tip dealers occasionally if you’re playing for a while and winning.[web:56]
  • Know your stop-loss before you sit down; walk away when you hit it.
Cheat Sheet
A screenshot-friendly summary of how to play most hands. Start here if you just want the quick rules.[web:26][web:60]

Quick decisions

Pairs

  • Split: A,A and 8,8.
  • Never split: 5,5 or 10,10.

Hard hands (no soft Ace)

  • 8 or less: Hit.
  • 9–11: Usually hit (advanced: double in good spots).[web:26][web:60]
  • 12–16: Stand vs dealer 2–6, hit vs 7–Ace.[web:57][web:58]
  • 17+: Stand.

Soft hands (Ace = 11)

  • Soft 13–17 (A+2 to A+6): Hit.[web:26][web:58]
  • Soft 18: Stand vs 2–8, hit vs 9–Ace.[web:26][web:58]
  • Soft 19+: Stand.
